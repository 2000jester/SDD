1. Posturue. Sit with your back straight and elbows at a 90 degree angle. adjust your chair height so that you only have to tilt your head forward to look at the screen slightly. Lastly keep your eyes about 45 - 70cm away from the screen.
2. Curve your fingers a little, and place them on the ASDF and JKL; keys. When typing your fingers should always return to these home keys, this is made easier by the small bumps on the F and J keys.
3. It is imperative to start at a slow pace when you first start learning to touch type. You need to allow time to develop muscle memory and get used to where all the keys are.
